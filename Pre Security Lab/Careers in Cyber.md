### Objective Achieved
I explored seven distinct career paths within cybersecurity, understanding the primary function, required skills, and key tools for each role. This provided a clear overview of how different teams contribute to an organization's security posture.

### Key Concepts Covered
* **Blue Team (Defensive) Roles:** Focus on protecting systems, detecting threats, and responding to incidents.
    * **Security Analyst:** Daily monitoring of security tools (like SIEM) to identify and triage threats.
    * **Security Engineer:** Designs, builds, and maintains the security infrastructure and tools.
    * **Incident Responder:** Manages the full lifecycle of a security breach, from containment to recovery.
    * **Digital Forensics Examiner:** Collects, preserves, and analyzes digital evidence post-incident for legal or technical purposes.
    * **Malware Analyst:** Investigates malicious software (malware) to understand its capabilities, origin, and impact.

* **Red Team (Offensive) Roles:** Focus on simulating attacks to find vulnerabilities before adversaries do.
    * **Penetration Tester:** Conducts authorized ethical hacks against systems to uncover security flaws.
    * **Red Teamer:** Conducts a more comprehensive, goal-based simulation of a real-world threat actor over an extended period.

* **Essential Skills:** Understanding that a core knowledge of networking, operating systems (Linux/Windows), and scripting (Python) is crucial across nearly all roles.
